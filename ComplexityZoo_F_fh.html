FH: Fourier Hierarchy

FH k {\displaystyle k} is the class of problems solvable by a uniform family of polynomial-size quantum circuits, with at most k {\displaystyle k} Fourier transforms (for k ∈ Z + {\displaystyle k\in \mathbb {Z} ^{+}} ), and all other gates preserving the computational basis. (Conditional phase flip gates are fine, for example.) Thus

  • FH0 = P
  • FH1 = BPP
  • FH2 contains factoring because of Kitaev's phase estimation algorithm

It is an open problem to show that the Fourier hierarchy is infinite relative to an oracle (that is, FH k {\displaystyle k} is strictly contained in FH k + 1 {\displaystyle k+1} ).

It is known that FH2 ⊆ {\displaystyle \subseteq } BQP
